I am a researcher working within project ENDORSE aiming at developing control methodologies for indor robotic fleet for healthcare and commercial applications. I am collaborating with the University of Thessaly, University of Cyprus and Citard, Nicosia, Cyprus.

From September 2017 to 2019 I worked as a postdoctoral researcher at the Systems and Control group, Laboratoire des Signaux et Systèmes and CNRS, Paris, France, collaborating with Prof. Antoine Girard. From January 2013 to June 2015, I held a postdoctoral position at the Department of Cardiac Surgery Boston Children’s Hospital, Harvard Medical School, MA, USA where I was collaborating with Prof. Pierre E. Dupont.

I received the MEng degree in Mechanical Engineering from National Technical University of Athens (NTUA) in 2004. In 2013, I received the PhD degree in Mechanical Engineering from the NTUA, Greece under the supervision of Prof. Kostas J. Kyriakopoulos and co-supervision of Dimos V. Dimarogonas from KTH, Sweden. My PhD Thesis was about Event-triggered Model Predictive Control regarding the development and implementation of robust control algorithms for single and multiple robotic agents under resource constraints.
